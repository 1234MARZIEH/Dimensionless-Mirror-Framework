# Abstract

In this work, we propose a compact framework based on dimensionless analysis
for rapid regime and stability identification in:

1. Bioreactors  
2. Gas–liquid absorption and separation systems  

Instead of solving full transport–reaction partial differential equations (PDEs),
we construct **Mirror Tables** that map key dimensionless groups
(Damköhler, Sherwood, Schmidt, Reynolds, Hatta numbers, etc.)
to operating regimes, stability conditions, and design decisions.

For bioreactors, the framework combines:
- Damköhler number (Da)
- Volumetric mass transfer coefficient (kLa)
- Oxygen transfer rate (OTR)
- Oxygen uptake rate (OUR)

to classify systems into:
- Kinetically controlled
- Mass-transfer-limited
- Potentially unstable regimes

For gas–liquid absorption systems, Sherwood, Reynolds,
Schmidt, and Hatta numbers are used to distinguish:

- Film-controlled
- Reaction-controlled
- Mixed regimes

The mirror tables enable rapid engineering screening without detailed numerical simulation.
